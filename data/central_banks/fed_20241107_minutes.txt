The Federal Open Market Committee (FOMC) held a meeting on November 6-7, 2024, where they reviewed recent developments in financial markets, the economic situation, and monetary policy actions. Here are the key points from the minutes:

1. **Financial Market Developments**: 
   - Treasury yields rose significantly due to stronger economic data and a shift in monetary policy expectations towards a more gradual easing. 
   - Equity prices increased, reflecting solid economic indicators and reduced fears of a downturn.
   - The federal funds rate path shifted upward, with market participants expecting a 25 basis point cut at the current meeting and another in December.

2. **Economic Situation**:
   - Real GDP growth was solid, although job gains had moderated, and the unemployment rate had slightly increased but remained low.
   - Consumer price inflation was below previous levels, with total PCE inflation at 2.1% and core inflation at 2.7%.
   - Labor market conditions were solid, with average hourly earnings rising but showing signs of moderation.

3. **International Developments**:
   - Policy rate expectations in advanced foreign economies declined, contributing to a stronger U.S. dollar.
   - Inflation abroad eased, with some countries experiencing increased inflation due to specific pressures.

4. **Monetary Policy Actions**:
   - The FOMC unanimously decided to lower the target range for the federal funds rate by 25 basis points to 4.5% - 4.75%.
   - The Committee emphasized the need to support maximum employment while continuing to work towards the 2% inflation target.

5. **Outlook and Risks**:
   - Participants expressed confidence that inflation was moving towards the 2% target but noted the potential for volatility in economic data.
   - Risks to the economic outlook were seen as balanced, with some concerns about global growth and financial conditions.

6. **Future Considerations**:
   - The Committee agreed to continue monitoring economic indicators closely and adjust monetary policy as necessary based on evolving conditions.

The next meeting is scheduled for December 17-18, 2024. Overall, the FOMC's discussions reflect a cautious yet optimistic outlook on the economy, with a focus on maintaining stability and addressing inflationary pressures.
